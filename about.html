Our story

Eleven years of quiet mornings on Alberta Street

Stillwater began in 2014 with six mats, a rented back room and one stubborn belief: yoga works best when it feels like coming home, not showing off.

How it started

Founder Elena Vasquez spent a decade teaching in big-box gyms where the rooms were loud, the lights were bright and the people who needed yoga most felt most out of place. She wanted the opposite: a small, warm room where the barista next door and the retired teacher down the block could practice side by side.

The first Stillwater room held eight students, if everyone breathed in at the same time. Word travelled the way it does in neighbourhoods — slowly, then all at once. In 2017 we took over the whole building, knocked through a wall, and built the three rooms we teach in today: the Sun Room, the Garden Room and the Moon Room.

We measure success in regulars, not in records.

How we teach

Every teacher on our roster has completed at least 500 hours of training and, more importantly, teaches the way we'd want to be taught: precise cues, real options for different bodies, and zero pressure to perform. Hands-on assists are always opt-in, signalled by a card on your mat.

We keep class sizes at eighteen, the rooms mirror-free, and the playlists quiet. The practice belongs to you — our job is to hold the room well enough that you can hear yourself think.

What we stand for

Four promises we make to every student

Every body welcome

All ages, sizes, abilities and experience levels. If a pose doesn't work for your body, we change the pose — never the other way around.

No mirrors, no judgement

Our rooms are deliberately mirror-free. You learn to feel alignment from the inside, and nobody's comparing — including you.

Tea before small talk

Arrive ten minutes early, stay ten minutes late. The lounge and the pot of chai are part of the practice — community is built in the in-between.

Accessible pricing

Community rates, work-trade and a quiet pay-what-you-can fund. Money should never be the reason someone can't practice.

Teachers

The people holding the room

Fourteen teachers total; these are the six you'll meet most often. Each brings their own lineage — and the same Stillwater promise.

Elena Vasquez

Founder · Vinyasa, Sunrise

Teaching since 2009. Trained in vinyasa and trauma-informed practice; believes the best class is the one you actually come to.

Marcus Chen

Power, Hatha

Former climber who came to yoga via a shoulder injury and stayed for the stillness. Expect strong classes and terrible puns.

Priya Nair

Restorative, Prenatal

Certified prenatal specialist and mother of two. Her candlelit restorative classes are the studio's worst-kept secret.

Sofia Lindqvist

Hatha, Slow Flow

Alignment nerd with a background in physiotherapy. She'll find the two-degree adjustment that changes everything.

Amara Okafor

Yin, Meditation

Studied contemplative practice in Kyoto and Cape Town. Teaches stillness like it's a superpower — because it is.

Jonah Reyes

Mat Pilates, Power

Comprehensive pilates certification and ten years of classes. His core sequences are legendary and only slightly cruel.
